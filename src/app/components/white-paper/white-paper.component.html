White Paper

Ebraj Corporation is an assets’ fund called (eBox). The primary goal of this fund is to serve as a source of financing for the Sustainable Social Solidarity System program, referred to hereafter as (4S).

The main purpose of the (4S) program is to provide partial support for individuals’ basic life needs, free of charge.

Reasons for Establishing the Program

We foresee future risks threatening the stability of the labor market, where a large number of individuals are expected to lose their jobs. With the absence of clear strategies to address these challenges and the continuous growth in the number of affected individuals, failing to develop a solution based on a sustainable and stable source could lead to threats to social and global security and peace. Therefore, it became imperative to design a program that seeks to offer practical solutions, enhancing the stability of individuals and communities in the long term.

How the Program Operates

The (4S) program operates through the provision of a digital wallet containing a balance of assets from the (eBox) fund. This wallet is granted to individuals free of charge after creating a verified account with Ebraj Corporation.

The spending conditions include the following:

  1. A portion allocated for housing.
  2. A portion allocated for food.
  3. A portion allocated for healthcare.
  4. A portion allocated for transportation.
  5. An optional portion.
  6. A portion allocated for investment (used as one of the mechanisms to recharge the wallet); and
  7. A portion allocated for savings.

The goal of this spending framework is to achieve a key objective: defining a standard known as the “Absolute Minimum for Living”, which establishes the minimum level of decent living for humans on Earth.

This mechanism also aims to enhance societal protection, ensuring that the quality of life does not fall below this minimum under any circumstances.

We believe that these spending conditions on essential needs grant individuals peace of mind and the ability to rise again with confidence to face life’s challenges3

Growth of the Fund

The principle of the fund’s wealth growth is straightforward: investing in income-generating developmental projects in exchange for obtaining a percentage of these projects.

The fund’s share percentage varies from one project to another based on several factors, including:

1. Investment Methods

  • direct Method

    • Investing directly in projects using the fund’s assets and resources, such as financial funding, service support, real estate owned by the fund, and more.

  • Indirect Method

    • Investment is made by providing a percentage of the fund’s assets as a guarantee to financing entities. These entities then provide direct funding to the project based on this guarantee. During its initial phase, the fund will rely exclusively on indirect investment to build sufficient assets, enabling future direct investments.

2. Decision-Maker for Investments

Investment decisions are made by the wallet holders themselves or through investment groups managing multiple wallets collectively.

It is essential to recognize that the fund’s growth will be a gradual process that occurs over several time phases, accompanied by varying challenges and difficulties depending on influencing factors. The (4S) program and the fund’s services will be launched gradually, either partially or fully, according to the available resources at each stage.

Ebraj Corporation Public Law

The Public Law serves as the company’s constitution and is one of the primary reasons individuals choose to join the “Ebraj” family. For this reason, all laws have been saved using blockchain technology, ensuring that each individual has an immutable and tamper-proof copy.

The Public Law is Divided Into Three Main Sections

1. Rules

Rules are fixed and unchangeable laws over time.

Examples of fixed rules:

  • Ebraj Corporation is a decentralized company.
  • No individual can directly own assets in Ebraj, regardless of the percentage of assets they hold.
  • No “Ebrajer” (subscriber) can be excluded from the company unless they voluntarily choose to relinquish their ownership (their share of the fund).
2. Policies

Policies are flexible laws that can be amended once every four years or less, provided a vote is held, and the majority approves the changes.

Examples of policies:

  • New investment funding must not exceed 30% of the total value of the fund.
  • The influence of an account holder remains effective for four full years after their account is deleted. The contract is renewed if they choose to revoke their withdrawal decision.
3. Conditions

Conditions are a set of laws that change based on specific factors and circumstances.

This section of laws will be developed gradually over time and will remain in a state of continuous development. The creation of this part of the laws is entrusted to the community and the audience. However, Ebraj Corporation is responsible for publishing these laws on its official website to ensure users can reference and benefit from them when needed.

Examples of conditions:

  • Determining the type of project that is legally permitted or refused for funding depends on local laws, societal culture, and other factors.
  • The decision to choose the appropriate method for evaluating a project is left to the members of the investment process.
